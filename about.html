About Cats

History and Domestication

Domestication: Cats (Felis catus) were domesticated around 7500 BC in the Near East1. They were valued for their ability to control vermin populations. Evolution: The first felinelike mammal, Proailurus, evolved about 30 million years ago2.

Physical Traits

Body: Cats have a strong, flexible body, quick reflexes, sharp teeth, and retractable claws adapted for hunting1. Senses: They have excellent night vision and a keen sense of smell1. Communication: Cats communicate through vocalizations like meowing, purring, and hissing, as well as through body language1.

Behavior

Social Structure: While cats are social animals, they are also solitary hunters1. Activity: They are crepuscular, meaning they are most active during dawn and dusk1.

Reproduction

Breeding: Female cats can have kittens from spring to late autumn in temperate zones and throughout the year in equatorial regions1. Litter sizes typically range from two to five kittens1.

Popularity

Pets: As of 2021, there were an estimated 220 million owned and 480 million stray cats worldwide1.
